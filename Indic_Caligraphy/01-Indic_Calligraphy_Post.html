How to write Indic Calligraphy

10/11/2014

Steps to learn Indic Calligraphy

Part 1:Choose content

Calligraphy is a type of handwriting and is also an art form, it should be mobilization and readability. A series of strokes are used to complete the letters. These letters are completed with a brush, a fountain pen, or a chimney filled with ink. Calligraphy is a skill that involves movement with hands, pressure, and touch. With a little practice you can write a good and correct calligraphy.

Gathering material To begin writing calligraphy, you should gather original material. You will need:

  • Writing a calligraphy writing such as a pen or brush
  • Ink, pen or pencil, you should choose the method that is most comfortable for you to write (pencil is for novices, but to get good calligraphy you need to learn to use pen and ink).
  • The paper
  • A smooth and firm support surface
  • A seat at a comfortable height
  • You can also use a ruler and a pencil (to draw lines for writing).

Choose a pen. For calligraphy, you have different pen options. You can use a felt calligraphy pen, a fountain or cartridge pen, or an immersion pen. All of them present good results with slight changes.

A felt calligraphy pen is similar to a marker. This type of pen is good for a beginner as it gives more control in the stroke. Harder tips are more predictable, while softer tips react to your hand as you get more advanced. These pens can easily be held on a horizontal surface. Choose the right point for jobs that are too wide and too big for thick strokes

The fountain pen has a tip pen, but it has an ink cartridge inside the pen. Fountains or cartridge pens can be used on horizontal surfaces, do not run out of ink in the middle of the word, and do not require open ink bottles. The nib is very rigid, so it does not respond and is not flexible whenever. If you have to change cartridges, they also spread, prevent, and mess up. They are good for beginners.

Dip pen is the original calligraphy pen which is related to the first feather made of tissue or feather feathers. To use an immersion pen, the pen is immersed in ink. For an immersion pen you need two items: a metal pen and a handle. An immersion pen can be used with Indian ink, ink drawing and acrylic ink - a fountain pen to plug and destroy other inks. Can also be used. There are pens of different sizes that you can get multiple lines of different widths. You can also change colors using different ink colors. An immersion pen is more difficult to use, but the most serious calligrants use it.

Choose Paper to Practice Given the type of ink used in calligraphy, simple paper is not the best option. Paper for a graph or printer is thin, so the ink goes through it. Any kind of plain paper can be used to practice, but if you want to make a finished product, you better choose paper. .

  • Use glued paper. This means that the paper has been made so that the ink is not absorbed by the paper fiber. It helps in getting clear and clear letters.
  • Get acid neutral pH paper. This type of paper is not yellow over time
  • Buy Heavy Paper Heavy paper allows a rigid surface for calligraphy. Calligraphy blocks range from 9 to 32 kg (20 to 70 lb). Start with at least 27 kg (60 lb) of paper to make the final projects. This is enough for most calligraphy projects.
  • There are three types of paper surfaces: hot pressed (smooth), cold pressed (to some extent), and rough (very hard). Smooth surfaces are generally good for calligraphy, although a bit of roughness may be good for retaining ink. Start with hot pressed paper at the beginning as you get more advanced and use an immersion pen. If so, try cold pressed paper.
  • If you do not have a lot of resources, you can buy a blog, blog, or a drawing blog, although their pages do not have as much quality as they can.
  • You can buy calligraphic pens and paper in most art shops or hobby shops.

Draw a line of practice on the paper. When you write calligraphy, you need to have straight lines to write. Use a ruler to draw a line on the paper. The height of your letter will depend on the width of your pen, if you are using an immersion pen.

Part 2:Learn to use an immersion pen

Dip the pen in ink, dip the end of the pen tip into the ink bottle. Leave it until it reaches three quarters from the tip. Tap the nib on the side of the ink bottle to shake off any excess ink.

  • If the pen does not have enough ink, it will run out faster. It will cause scratching lines. You also have to stop to fill. If there is too much ink, it can spread, which will cause dripping or inkjet.
  • Hold the pen horizontally after it sinks. Place the ink on the paper to prevent it from falling on top of you.
  • Test the ink flow of the pen on a separate sheet before starting.

Hold the pen horizontally. Take the pen with your writing hand. Do not hold the pen with too much force. This can lead to trembling letters making sure you are sitting comfortably. If you wish, you can write on a surface that is slightly tilted up to 30 degrees. Grab the pen like you would a normal pen

Although held as a pen, it is not written in the same way as you would with a normal pen. The pressure will be different and you will use different strokes than normal writing.

To get the best ink release, place the pen horizontally against the paper at an angle of 20 to 45 degrees. Keep pen angle constant while typing.

Write with an immersion pen. When you have filled the pen and placed it on paper, there will be time to write with it. The pressure you apply varies according to the type of stroke you have done. To make a downward stroke (top to bottom), use gentle pressure so that the pen touches the paper hard. If you press too hard on one upward stroke, you will get a splash of ink.

Part 3:Learn basic calligraphy writing

Understand the letter. With normal writing, calligraphy writing has both uppercase and lowercase letters. The space on the page where they are drawn is very accurate. There are three areas that you need to know when you think about typing in writing.

  • The baseline is the following parallel line. All the letters on this line are left. You should always return to this
  • The upper line is the parallel line above where all higher letters reach.
  • The middle line is halfway between the baseline and the climbing line. It may be invisible on the practice sheet or it may be a dotted line. This is where lowercase letters arrive
  • Lineage lines are of similar thickness from base to midline. Here is where all the characters coming down from the baseline reach.

Learn the descent line The descent line is one of the two main lines in calligraphy. This is one of the first things that you have to practice and correct. Before practicing this stroke, use some practice sheets, just draw lines, curves and curls. Feel the pen and paper while practicing pen filling

Place the pen on the top row, with the tip of the pen flat on the paper, move your hand down. Do not keep the line straight - instead, it is better to tilt the line to the right if you are on the right. If you are surdo, this lean line to the left should increase from the upper line to the lower line.

When you finish there should be a short tail down.

Stroke the entire sheet from top to bottom. Working to improve your lines and using how the pen feels.

Learn the basic curve The basic curve is the second main line in calligraphy. Basic curve looks like a c lowercase.

  • Start with the tip of the pen in the middle of two parallel lines. If you make a dotted line between the parallel lines, start from there.
  • Draw a curve with the pen down and to the left when you reach the bottom line.
  • Practice filling a full page with a basic curve.

Practice other basic strokes. Draw parallel lines instead of down. Place the pen in the same angle and position as you draw the stroke down and the curve.

  • Later practice the diagonal line. Make sure you keep the pen in the same position, no matter how you go. Make a diagonal from right to left. This will create a very thin line. Then, make a diagonal from left to right. A wider stroke will be produced.

Write some letters After acquiring the basics, learn some letters. Letters are made from very strokes put together. Practice basic strokes before beginning to learn alphabets.
