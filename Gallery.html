  • Qamar Dagar - a famous calligraphy woman in India - was really creative when blending traditional calligraphy with modern images.

    India has 22 different languages, but the real beauty is not just hearing them speak, but seeing them perform in writing. The bustling streets of Delhi's Urdu Bazaar shopping district were once crowded with long lines waiting for calligraphy. The giver sat on the steps of the Jama mosque and put his pen on the paper. They chatted, enjoyed tea and some curious tourists watched them work. In today's modern society, only a few people do this work. One of them, the famous calligraphy house - Qamar Dagar in New Delhi - is fighting to preserve the tradition that exists in an increasingly digitalized era.

  • Qamar Dagar - a famous calligraphy woman in India - was really creative when blending traditional calligraphy with modern images.

    "Calligraphy is a spiritual practice," explained Ms. Dagar, "because it allows one to understand oneself and to train self-discipline through this medium." At the same time, it is also a form. express yourself. Ms. Dagar uses an abstract style - called picture calligraphy - that combines words and pictures to reveal personal understanding of the words being depicted.

  • The lingering charm of Chinese calligraphy

    Since the invention of the "Oracle bone script," Chinese calligraphy has experienced several development stages, from "Seal script" to "Clerical script," "Cursive script," "Regular script" and "Running script." There are a large number of calligraphers and calligraphic works that were produced and helped constitute a profound tradition of Chinese calligraphy at each stage.

  • Modern Interpretations

    Following the rule of Chairman Mao and the Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution, during which old culture and customs were banished, the ancient artform was altered as younger generations didn’t have a pervasive knowledge of the classic form. Later in the 20th century, President Xiaoping pursued a cultural recovery encouraging a calligraphy comeback.
